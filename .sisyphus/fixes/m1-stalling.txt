M1 STALLING PREVENTION — TWO-COUNTER FIX
Rulebook §8.5: "If no player evaluates their function within 20 turns globally
OR if five consecutive turns pass without any evaluation, all players must
evaluate at the next opportunity."

BUG: Single counter `stalling_no_eval_turns` resets on eval, making the
20-global-turn mechanism unreachable. Fix: split into TWO counters with
different reset policies.

GLOBAL-CAP SEMANTICS (decided):
  - `global_no_eval_turns` caps at 20 and NEVER resets.
  - Once global reaches 20, force-eval fires on THAT turn.
  - After force-eval resolves (an evaluation happened), the global counter
    stays at 20 but no further force-eval fires until 5 MORE consecutive
    no-eval turns accumulate (via the consecutive counter).
  - If the game reaches global=20 AGAIN (hypothetical — can't happen since
    it never resets), it would just stay at 20 with no re-trigger because
    the eval already occurred.

RESET SEMANTICS (decided):
  - `consecutive_no_eval_turns` resets to 0 ONLY on a SUCCESSFUL eval
    (not on play, not on a rejected eval, not on attack). Per rulebook
    "pass without any evaluation" — only an evaluation clears the streak.
  - `global_no_eval_turns` NEVER resets.
